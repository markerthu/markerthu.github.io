CESAR: Consistent, Effective, and Scalable Audio Reasoners

TL;DR ‚Äî Audio LLMs trained only on outcome rewards produce hallucinatory, inconsistent, and unscalable reasoning chains. CESAR shifts to rewarding the reasoning process itself via online RL (GRPO), resolving test-time inverse scaling and achieving SOTA on MMAU ‚Äî outperforming Gemini 2.5 Pro and GPT-4o Audio.

üîç The Problem

Adding chain-of-thought reasoning to Audio LLMs often degrades performance ‚Äî a phenomenon we term test-time inverse scaling. Longer reasoning chains yield progressively worse results. Why?

‚ùå Without CESAR

Models without guidance for the reasoning process produce hallucinatory, inconsistent reasoning that accumulates errors over longer chains. Outcome-only rewards cannot fix this.

‚úÖ With CESAR

Process rewards incentivize consistency, structured analytical patterns, causal reasoning, and calibrated depth ‚Äî transforming reasoning from a liability into a genuine capability.

‚öôÔ∏è Method

CESAR uses Group Relative Policy Optimization (GRPO) with a multi-faceted reward suite that goes beyond simple correctness:

1

Correctness Reward

Standard outcome reward ‚Äî is the final answer right?

2

Consistency Reward

Does the reasoning chain stay internally consistent?

3

Structure Reward

Does the reasoning follow structured analytical patterns and causal logic?

4

Depth Calibration

Is the reasoning depth calibrated to task complexity? Avoid over- and under-thinking.
